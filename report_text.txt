Abstract
The easy availability of the internet has caused an expansion of fake news being
disseminated through online media. This widespread dissemination has led to an
explosion of misinformation, which can significantly shape public views and influence
critical decisions. Fake news in regional languages like Malayalam poses unique
challenges due to limited datasets, di fficulties in processing the script, and the lack
of advanced detection tools tailored for such languages. To address these challenges,
this project aims to build an e ffective Malayalam fake news detection system using
sophisticated deep learning models such as Bidirectional Long Short-Term Memory
(BiLSTM), Multilingual BERT (mBERT), and Gated Recurrent Units (GRU). The
primary goal is to design a high-accuracy model capable of classifying Malayalam
news as real or fake, thereby combating the spread of misinformation. Additionally,
a free API will be developed to enable real-time identification of false news, ensuring
accessibility and practical utility for users.
Keywords: deep learning, mBERT, BiLSTM, GRU, transformers, NLP, misinforma-
tion, fake news

Chapter 1
Introduction
In today’s digital age, fake news has emerged as a significant societal challenge. It
spreads misinformation, creates unwarranted panic, fuels disputes, and often obscures
critical facts. Unfortunately, fake news is easily accepted and shared by individuals
without realizing its damaging consequences. Misinformation is frequently exploited
to manipulate public opinion, misguide voters during elections, or incite social unrest.
While fake news detection systems are well-established for dominant languages
like English, detecting fake news in regional languages such as Malayalam remains
a daunting task. The primary challenges include limited datasets, di fficulties in
processing the Malayalam script, and achieving high accuracy in classification.
To address these challenges, our system leverages advanced artificial intelligence
(AI) and machine learning (ML) techniques. Specifically, we employ Bidirectional
Long Short-Term Memory (BiLSTM), multilingual BERT (mBERT), and Gated Re-
current Units (GRU) to analyze Malayalam text e ffectively and detect false news with
improved accuracy. Additionally, API integration enables real-time authentication,
empowering users to verify the authenticity of news at any time with ease.
A brief review of existing works reveals that while several models have been
developed for fake news detection in widely spoken languages, there is a notable
lack of research and tools tailored for regional languages like Malayalam. Traditional
approaches often rely on rule-based methods or simplistic statistical models, which
1
struggle to handle the nuances of local languages. Our work aims to bridge this gap by
introducing a robust and scalable solution specifically designed for Malayalam.
1.1 Motivation
The proliferation of fake news poses serious consequences, including the spread
of false rumors, unfair influence over people, and the creation of fear among the
public. This issue is particularly acute in regions where local languages dominate,
as misinformation spreads unchecked due to the absence of e ffective detection
mechanisms. Our system is motivated by the urgent need to empower individuals
to recognize and stop the spread of fake news in Malayalam.
The purpose of this system is twofold: first, to detect fake news accurately; and
second, to prevent its dissemination. By enabling users to report false news easily
and ensuring that only verified information is circulated, we aim to foster a more
informed and responsible society. With this tool, anyone can authenticate the veracity
of news with just a few clicks. By making it freely accessible and user-friendly, we
encourage individuals to critically evaluate the information they consume and share,
thereby reducing misinformation and promoting awareness about the dangers of fake
news.
1.2 Objective
The primary objectives of this system are as follows:
1. To develop a robust AI-driven model capable of detecting fake news in the
Malayalam language with high accuracy.
2. To provide real-time authentication of news through API integration, ensuring
accessibility and ease of use.
3. To create a platform where users can report suspected fake news, fostering
community involvement in combating misinformation.
2
4. To educate and raise awareness among users about the importance of verifying
information before sharing it online.
By achieving these objectives, we aim to mitigate the adverse e ffects of fake news
and promote a culture of truthfulness and accountability in the digital space.
1.3 Contributions
The key contributions of this work are summarized below:
•Development of a novel AI-based framework utilizing BiLSTM, mBERT, and
GRU for fake news detection in Malayalam.
•Addressing the unique challenges posed by the Malayalam script and limited

•Integration of a real-time API for seamless and instant verification of news
articles.
•Creation of a user-friendly interface that allows individuals to verify news
authenticity and report false information e ffortlessly.
•Fostering community engagement and awareness about the dangers of misinfor-
mation in regional languages.
These contributions collectively aim to set a new benchmark for fake news
detection in local languages and inspire further research in this domain.

Chapter 2
Literature Review
This chapter provides an overview of recent advancements in Malayalam fake news
detection. It highlights various machine learning (ML) and deep learning (DL)
approaches that have been employed to address this challenge. The literature review is
organized into sections covering traditional ML methods, transformer-based models,
hybrid deep learning models, and key observations. These studies collectively
contribute to understanding the strengths and limitations of current techniques in
detecting fake news in Malayalam.
2.1 Machine Learning Approaches
The use of machine learning techniques has been a foundational approach in Malay-
alam fake news detection. Coelho et al introduced a machine learning-based approach
for detecting fake news in Malayalam. Their work focused on leveraging traditional
ML models such as Multinomial Na ¨ıve Bayes (MNB), Logistic Regression (LR), and
Support Vector Machines (SVM). They achieved significant accuracy using TF-IDF
word unigrams for feature extraction. This study was part of the DravidianLangTech
2023 proceedings [1].
Anit Sara Santhosh and Bijimol developed a machine learning model for identify-
ing fake news in Malayalam. Their approach used the Passive Aggressive Classifier
with TF-IDF features. The dataset consisted of 316 Malayalam news headlines, and
the model achieved 98.4% accuracy. However, the small dataset size and focus on
5
headlines limited its applicability to full articles [2].
2.2 Transformer-Based Approaches
Transformer-based models have shown significant promise in processing Malayalam
text for fake news detection. Subramanian et al presented a comprehensive approach
to combating Malayalam fake news. They introduced a new dataset where fake news
was categorized into multiple levels: False, Partly False, Mostly False, and Half True.
Their optimal model used Logistic Regression with LaBSE embeddings, achieving
an F1-score of 0.3393. This work was published in the DravidianLangTech 2024
proceedings [3].
Eduri Raja et al. optimized the XLM-RoBERTa model for binary fake news
classification in Malayalam. Their fine-tuned model achieved an F1-score of 87% and
ranked 2nd in the DravidianLangTech 2023 competition. This study demonstrated the
effectiveness of transformer-based models in handling code-mixed Malayalam text [4].
Farsi et al. addressed both binary and multi-class fake news classification in
Malayalam using fine-tuned MuRIL BERT. For the binary task, their model achieved
an F1-score of 86% (ranked 3rd), while for the multi-class task, it achieved an F1-
score of 51.91% (ranked 1st). This work highlighted the importance of fine-tuning
transformer models for regional languages like Malayalam [5].
Hariharan and Anand Kumar explored the impact of focal loss on transformer-
based fake news detection in Malayalam. They fine-tuned models such as mBERT,
XLM-RoBERTa, and MuRIL. The MuRIL model achieved the highest F1-score
of 87%, placing it 2nd in the DravidianLangTech 2023 competition. This study
emphasized the role of focal loss in improving model performance for imbalanced
datasets [6].
6
2.3 Hybrid Deep Learning Models
Hybrid models combine traditional embeddings with deep learning architectures,
offering state-of-the-art results. Hashmi et al. introduced a hybrid deep learning
model that integrates FastText embeddings with CNN, LSTM, and CNN-LSTM
architectures. Their CNN-LSTM model with FastText embeddings achieved 99%
accuracy, surpassing transformer models like BERT, XLNet, and RoBERTa. This
study, published in IEEE Access , also incorporated explainable AI techniques to
interpret the model’s predictions [7].
2.4 Key Observations and Challenges
Several strengths emerge from the reviewed studies: - Ensemble ML models sig-
nificantly improved classification accuracy, as demonstrated by Coelho et al [1]. -
Transformer models, such as MuRIL and XLM-RoBERTa, demonstrated exceptional
performance in processing Malayalam text, as shown by Farsi et al. [5] and Eduri
Raja et al [4]. - Hybrid deep learning models, such as CNN-LSTM with FastText
embeddings, produced state-of-the-art results with 99% accuracy, as reported by
Hashmi et al [7].
Despite their strengths, these approaches face several challenges:
- Dataset imbalance significantly impacted multi-class classification performance.
- Transformer-based models incurred high computational costs.
- Limited generalization to real-world scenarios due to reliance on specific datasets.

3.1 Overview of Key Models and API
To detect fake news in Malayalam, we employed three state-of-the-art models:
Multilingual BERT (mBERT), Bidirectional Long Short-Term Memory (BiLSTM),
and Gated Recurrent Unit (GRU). Additionally, an API was integrated to enable real-
time fake news detection. Below is a detailed overview of each model and the API.
3.1.1 Multilingual BERT (mBERT)
Multilingual BERT (mBERT) is a transformer-based pre-trained language model
designed to handle multiple languages, including Malayalam. It leverages contextual
embeddings to capture the meaning of words based on their surrounding text. Fine-
tuning mBERT on a labeled Malayalam dataset allows it to classify news as real or
fake with high accuracy [8].

and attention mechanisms.

Advantages
- Captures contextual information e ffectively due to its transformer architecture.
- Generalizes well across multiple languages, making it suitable for low-resource
languages like Malayalam.
- Achieves state-of-the-art performance in text classification tasks.
Disadvantages
- Computationally expensive, requiring significant resources for training and inference.
- Requires large datasets for fine-tuning to achieve optimal performance.
Use Case
mBERT is ideal for scenarios where high accuracy is critical, and computational
resources are not a limiting factor.
3.1.2 Bidirectional Long Short-Term Memory (BiLSTM)
Bidirectional Long Short-Term Memory (BiLSTM) is a type of recurrent neural
network (RNN) that processes sequences in both forward and backward directions.
This bidirectional approach enables the model to capture contextual information from
both past and future inputs, making it suitable for tasks like text classification [9].
9

Advantages
- Captures sequential dependencies in text e ffectively.
- Balances performance and computational e fficiency compared to transformer
models.
Disadvantages
- Struggles with very long sequences due to vanishing gradient issues.
- Less e ffective at capturing global context compared to transformer models.
Use Case
BiLSTM is suitable for smaller datasets and scenarios where computational resources
are limited.
10
3.1.3 Gated Recurrent Unit (GRU)
Gated Recurrent Unit (GRU) is a simplified variant of LSTM that reduces com-
putational complexity while maintaining similar performance. GRUs use gating
mechanisms to control the flow of information, allowing them to process long
sequences e fficiently [10].

gates.
Figure 3.3: Architecture of Gated Recurrent Unit (GRU)
Advantages
- Faster training and inference compared to LSTM and BiLSTM.
- Requires fewer parameters, making it more lightweight.
11
Disadvantages
- May underperform on tasks requiring complex contextual understanding.
- Less robust than transformer models for multi-language support.
Use Case
GRU is ideal for lightweight applications and scenarios where computational e fficiency
is prioritized over absolute accuracy.

The API is designed for real-world applications where users need instant verification

4.3 Comparison with Previous Work
In our previous work, the Bidirectional LSTM (BiLSTM) model was employed for
fake news detection in Malayalam. While the BiLSTM model achieved an accuracy of
72%, it faced challenges in handling contextual dependencies and capturing nuanced
linguistic patterns. The model struggled particularly with code-mixed text and dialectal
variations, leading to misclassifications in certain cases.
In contrast, the updated work leverages Multilingual BERT (mBERT), a transformer-
based model pre-trained on multiple languages. Fine-tuning mBERT on the Malay-
alam dataset significantly improved the system’s performance, achieving an accuracy
of 93.54%. This represents a substantial improvement over the previous model,
particularly in handling complex linguistic structures and classifying news articles
more accurately.

4.4 Discussion
The results indicate that transformer-based models like mBERT outperform traditional
recurrent neural network architectures such as GRU and BiLSTM. This can be
attributed to the pre-trained embeddings and contextual understanding provided by
transformer models. However, the computational cost of mBERT is higher compared
to GRU and BiLSTM, making it less suitable for resource-constrained environments.
4.4.1 Strengths
- Transformer-based models (e.g., mBERT) achieve higher accuracy and F1-scores.
- BiLSTM and GRU models provide a good balance between performance and
computational e fficiency. - The use of confusion matrices allows for detailed analysis
of classification errors.
4.4.2 Limitations
- Limited availability of labeled datasets for Malayalam fake news detection. -
Transformer models require significant computational resources for training and
inference. - Imbalanced datasets may a ffect model performance, particularly for
minority classes.
